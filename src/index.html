ROBOTICS

My competitive robotics journey started back in 2018, with me joining Team Polaris and participating in the FIRST Lego League. The FIRST Lego League taught me many aspects of robotics and competition in general, such as programming algorithms, mechanical design, and keeping cool in high stress situations. In 2019, I moved to the VEX Robotics Competition on team 839 Caution Tape Robotics. VRC brought me deeper in the world of STEM and robotics, with the game being more challenging and the competition being fiercer. In a way, robotics has brought me to enjoy programming and design. In 2022 I moved to the new Silver Owl Robotics VRC team, 3150A. After having a successful stint at Silver Owl Robotics winning the 2022 Ontario Provincial Championship, I decided to create my own team for the new VRC season, Team 25K Robotics.


In VRC, I learned the language of C++ and continue to develop my skills in that. As the head programmer on VRC Team 839A before, I managed our libraries of code for our robot. I collaborated with other programmers on Team 839A to solve problems and find new solutions to challenges we are presented to in VRC. On the team I also worked on CAD in Fusion 360, to design and manufacture new parts for the robot. I also got into a new field of marketing at 3150A, with video production and graphics creation. Now on Team 25K, I manage the team and also design parts for our robot.

I am interested in pursuing a future in digital design or communication technology. There is a program of particular interest for me at the University of Toronto, Communication, Culture, Information & Technology (CCIT). This program will let me gain more knowledge behind media. For this program, grade 12 English is required as well as 6 other grade 12 courses. The average grade is mid to high 70s.


There are many pros and cons of this program. The program is related to what I want to do in the future, which is good. It also takes place at the University of Toronto, one of the best in Canada. However, there is a test after the first year which you must pass in order to continue in the program. The careers from the program include UI/UX designer, game developer, graphics designer, and digital marketing.
